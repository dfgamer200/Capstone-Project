Vehicle Troubleshooting Guide

Below are some of the most common issues drivers experience and how you can diagnose and fix them.

1. Engine Won’t Start

Common Causes:

  • Dead or weak battery
  • Corroded battery terminals
  • Faulty starter motor
  • No fuel or a clogged fuel filter

How to Fix:

  • Check battery voltage or attempt a jump-start
  • Clean corrosion from the terminals
  • Listen for clicking (possible starter issue)
  • Ensure the vehicle has enough fuel
  • Replace the fuel filter if clogged

2. Check Engine Light Is On

Common Causes:

  • Loose or faulty gas cap
  • Bad oxygen sensor
  • Mass airflow sensor malfunction
  • Catalytic converter issues

How to Fix:

  • Tighten or replace the gas cap
  • Read trouble codes using an OBD-II scanner
  • Replace failing sensors as indicated
  • Visit a mechanic if the issue persists

3. Engine Overheating

Common Causes:

  • Low coolant level
  • Faulty thermostat
  • Radiator blockage or leak
  • Cooling fan failure

How to Fix:

  • Refill coolant to the proper level
  • Inspect for leaks in hoses or radiator
  • Replace the thermostat
  • Ensure cooling fans are operating correctly

4. Strange Noises While Driving

Common Causes:

  • Squealing brakes
  • Loose belts or pulleys
  • Worn suspension components
  • Failing wheel bearings

How to Fix:

  • Inspect and replace brake pads/rotors
  • Retighten or replace worn belts
  • Have suspension checked for wear
  • Replace damaged wheel bearings

5. Battery Draining Quickly

Common Causes:

  • Failing alternator
  • Parasitic electrical drain
  • Aging battery

How to Fix:

  • Test alternator voltage output
  • Ensure lights/electronics are turned off
  • Perform a parasitic draw test
  • Replace older batteries (4–5+ years)

When unsure about a repair, always consult a certified mechanic for safety and accuracy.
